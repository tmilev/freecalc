$\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{x^{2}+3 x+1}{x^{2}+2 x}$. \textbf{For this problem, indicate only the $x$-coordinates of the local maxima/minima and inflection points; you do not need to compute the $y$-coordinates of those points.} 

Computation shows that 
$\displaystyle
f'(x)= \frac{- x^{2}-2 x-2}{\left(x^{2}+2 x\right)^{2}} 
$
and that 
$\displaystyle f''(x)= \frac{2 x^{3}+6 x^{2}+12 x+8}{\left(x^{2}+2 x\right)^{3}} =\frac{\left(x+1\right) \left(2 x^{2}+4 x+8\right)}{\left(x^{2}+2 x\right)^{3}} $; you may use those computations without further justification.

\answer{
\begin{tabular}{l}
