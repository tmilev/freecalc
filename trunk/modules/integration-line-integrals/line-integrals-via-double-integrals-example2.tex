Integrate 

$\displaystyle \int_{C} \left(y^3+  e^{\Arctan x}\right)\diff x +\left(-x^3+\ln \left(\cos y+y+4\right) \right)\diff y $,

where $C$ is the oriented boundary of the disk $D$ with radius $2$ and centered at the origin.
\end{columns}
\uncover<2->{
Direct computation of the line integral appears intractable. Since $P,Q$ are smooth over $D$ we can use Green's theorem. This makes sense as $P_y,Q_x$ are simple expressions.
\hfil \hfil $
\begin{array}{rcll|l}
\displaystyle \int_C P\diff x +Q\diff y&=&\displaystyle \int_{D} (Q_x-P_y)\diff x \diff y&&\text{Green's Thm.}\\
&=&\displaystyle \int_{D}\left(-3x^2-3y^2\right)\diff x \diff y &&\text{use polar coords.}\\
&=&\displaystyle \int_{\theta=0}^{2\pi}\int_{r=0}^{2} (-3r^2 )r\diff r \diff \theta\\
&=&\displaystyle \int_{\theta=0}^{2\pi}\left[ \frac{3}{4} r^4 \right]_{r=0}^{r=2} \diff \theta= \displaystyle  24\pi \quad .
\end{array}
$
}
