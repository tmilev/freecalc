\begin{example}[Example 9, p. 338]
Since $f(x) = (\tan x)/ (1 + x^2 + x^4) $ satisfies $f(-x) = -f(x)$, it is odd, and so
\begin{eqnarray*}
\int_{-1}^1 \frac{\tan x}{1+x^2+x^4} \diff x%
& = &%
\uncover<2->{%
0%
}%
\end{eqnarray*}
\end{example}
