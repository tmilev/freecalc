\begin{definition}[Vertical Asymptote]
The line $x = a$ is called a vertical asymptote of the curve $y = f(x)$ if at least one of the following statements is true:
\[
\begin{array}{lll}
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a}f(x) = \infty &
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a^-}f(x) = \infty &
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a^+}f(x) = \infty \\
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a}f(x) = -\infty &
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a^-}f(x) = -\infty &
\displaystyle \lim_{x\rightarrow a^+}f(x) = -\infty 
\end{array}
\]
\end{definition}
