\solution{\ref{problem2ln(2)-3ln(3)+4ln(4)}.
\begin{align*}
2\ln 2 - 3\ln 3 + 4\ln 4 & = \ln 2^2 - \ln 3^3 + \ln 4^4 \\
 & = \ln 4 - \ln 27 + \ln 256 \\
 & = \ln \Big( \frac{4}{27}\Big) + \ln 256 \\
 & = \ln \Big( \frac{4\cdot 256}{27}\Big) \\
 & = \ln \Big( \frac{1024}{27}\Big).
\end{align*}

$\frac{1024}{27}$ is not a rational power of $e$, therefore$ \ln \left( \frac{1024}{27}\right)$ is not a rational number and there are no further simplifications of our answer (without giving a numerical approximation using a calculator or equivalent techniques).
