% begin module not-product-rule
\begin{frame}
We need a formula for the derivative of the product of two functions.  One might guess that the derivative of a product is the product of the derivatives; however, this is wrong.
\uncover<2->{%
\begin{example}[Not the Product Rule]
Let $\alertNoH{3,4}{ f(x) = x}$ and $\alertNoH{5,6}{g(x) = x^2}$.
\abovedisplayskip=0pt
\belowdisplayskip=0pt
\abovedisplayshortskip=0pt
\belowdisplayshortskip=0pt
\begin{align*}
  \alertNoH{ 3-4}{f'(x)} & \alertNoH{ 3-4}{ = \fcAnswer{4}{1.}}  %
& \alertNoH{ 9-10,11,12}{(fg)(x)} &  \alertNoH{ 9-10}{ =f(x)g(x) \alertNoH{11,12}{= \fcAnswer{10}{x^3.}}}\\%
  \alertNoH{ 5-6}{g'(x)} & \alertNoH{ 5-6}{ = \fcAnswer{6}{2x.}}  %
& \alertNoH{ 11-12,15}{(fg)'(x)} & \alertNoH{ 11-12,15}{ = \fcAnswer{12}{3x^2.}}\\%
\alertNoH{ 7-8,14}{f'(x)g'(x)} & \alertNoH{ 7-8,14}{ = \fcAnswer{8}{2x.}}  %
&  & %
\end{align*}
\uncover<13->{%
Therefore
\[\alertNoH{13}{\alertNoH{14}{ f'(x)g'(x)} \neq \alertNoH{15}{(fg)'(x)}}\quad .
\]%
}%
\end{example}
}%
\uncover<16->{%
The correct formula is called the Product Rule.
}%
\end{frame}
